Freenet For Dummies

Windows 95/98/2000 only

 

Installing and running Freenet on your system is easier than ever.

Read through these simple steps, then follow them carefully.

Step 1 Download and install a "Java Runtime Environment".
This is another program that Freenet needs to be able to work.
Click this button:
and choose 'Run this program from its current location'
Step 2 When the Java Runtime Environment starts installing, follow the directions.
Step 3 Download Freenet: Click here.
When the second window comes up, click on a link marked 'Windows Installer', and choose "Run this program from its current location".

Step 4

 Uncheck the option
"Launch Freenet at Windows startup"
and then click 'Next'.
Choose the install directory, then again click "Next".
Step 5 You'll see a window titled
"Locating Java Runtimes"
When this window settles down, click on the line that contains the word "JavaSoft",
then click the "Update" button
You can accept all the other settings.

Congratulations - you're now running Freenet, and you are now a member of this revolutionary anonymous file-sharing community.

So How Do I Use Freenet?

You'll see a little icon - a grey rabbit - in your task tray at the bottom right of your screen.
Double-click on this icon.
Your browser should start up showing a page titled "Freenet Gateway"

So how do I download stuff from Freenet?

Everything that's stored in freenet has a special "name" (we call this a "Freenet key").
If you don't know the key, you can't get the file.

What the Hell's a Freenet Key?

A Freenet key is a funny-looking jumble of characters - it's like a 'combination lock' that lets you get stuff.

Here's an example of freenet keys:

SSK@Xdxr9gtIHEasJ1QrGK1T1Wr30kUQAgE/index-20010204.html
SSK@j9-x~6R0AAT8wGv0XWqgbIBOODIQAgE/blowjob.mpg
KSK@docs/books/english/religion/christian/holy_bible_KJV.zip

So how do I get a list of Freenet keys?

There are three main ways.

1) Follow the links on the 'Freenet Gateway' page, or

2) Search for "Freenet keys" in any search engine, or

3) Find other people who use Freenet, and ask them for their keys.

So, now I've got some Freenet Keys - how do I download stuff?

Two ways.

1) Open up your web browser and, in the Address box, type in:

http://127.0.0.1:8081/

Then straight after that, without not even a space, type (or paste) your freenet key.

For example, if your freenet key is:
KSK@docs/books/english/fiction/george_orwell/1984.html
you would type into your browser the address:
http://127.0.0.1:8081/KSK@docs/books/english/fiction/george_orwell/1984.html

<don't forget to press the "Enter" key>

OR

2) If your browser is showing the Freenet Gateway page, you can try typing the key into the "Request Data" form and click the "Request" button

Wow! This Is Cool!
But How Do I Put Stuff Into Freenet?

The most reliable way at the moment is:

1) Find where your file is on your PC, and remember its location

In the example below, we'll pretend there's a file called:
c:\pics\britfelch.jpg

2) Choose a Freenet Key for your file.

To do this, choose a unique name that you want your file to be known as on Freenet.
Make sure this name gives people an idea of what the file is.

For example, you might want to call this file Britney Spears Felch,
Then, your Freenet key would be:
KSK@Britney-Spears-Felch.jpg

(To keep things simple, you can make any key with KSK@ followed by any sequence of letters,numbers, full-stop or dash. KSK@ is a magic word that simple Freenet keys need to start with.)

3) Click on the 'Start' button at the bottom left of your screen

4) Click on "Run.."

5) Type in "command", then click OK

6) When the black DOS screen shows up, type in:

c:
cd \progra~1\freenet
finsert freenet:KSK@Britney-Spears-Felch.jpg C:\pics\britfelch.jpg

(Note - there are only 3 command lines above. If you see 4 lines, then maximise your browser window.)

7) Wait till the black window shows :

"C:\Progra~1\Freenet>"

8) Congratulations!

You've successfully inserted your first Freenet key, and it's now being 'uploaded' to other computers on Freenet.. But look at the little "two computers" icon at the bottom right of the screen - wait till they stop flashing blue or green before you close your internet connection.

When the upload is complete, you can send the key to all of your friends.
